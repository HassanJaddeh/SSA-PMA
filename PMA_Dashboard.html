Saves, Reach, Impressions, and Video Views are available in native platform exports but are not used as primary engagement metrics in this analysis — they are treated as secondary reach indicators because they are not available from public scrapes, making cross-period comparisons inconsistent.

Normalisation & comparability

Where native exports and public scrapes overlap for the same period, native export data takes precedence. The scrape data is used to fill coverage gaps (pre-2024) and to provide comment text for sentiment analysis.

Engagement averages are calculated at the post level (not page level) unless explicitly stated. This means that pages with fewer, higher-performing posts are not penalised by volume.

All date parsing uses dayfirst=True for Palestine-formatted timestamps (DD/MM/YYYY HH:MM). Instagram ISO timestamps are UTC-converted before analysis. LinkedIn scrape dates are UTC. This has been validated against known event dates in the data.

LinkedIn impressions are entirely absent from the public scrape dataset (all null values). Engagement rate (eng / impressions) cannot be computed from scrape data for LinkedIn — only from the native exports available from Apr 2025 onward.

4  ·  Sentiment analysis — methodology & assumptions

Classifier design

A rule-based Arabic sentiment classifier was built from scratch for this project, given that transformer-based Arabic NLP models (CAMeL-Lab, AraBERT) could not be loaded in the offline analysis environment. The classifier operates on three signal types applied in parallel:

① Arabic Lexicon (primary signal)

80+ curated positive and 40+ negative Arabic terms selected for PMA's financial authority context. Positive terms include gratitude, praise, and endorsement vocabulary. Negative terms cover fraud accusations, service failure complaints, religious financial objections (حرام), and policy criticism. Each matched term contributes ±2 to the raw score.

② Emoji Signal Detection (secondary signal)

25 positive emoji (👏 ❤️ 🔥 💪 ✅ 🙏 etc.) and 8 negative emoji (😡 👎 😤 💔 etc.) contribute ±1.5 per occurrence to the raw score. Emoji-only comments (common on Instagram) are fully classifiable through this signal alone.

③ VADER (Latin-script fallback)

For any Latin-script content within a comment (English words, mixed-language), the VADER sentiment analyser contributes its compound score (if |compound| > 0.05). This handles bilingual comments without misclassifying Arabic-dominant text.

Classification logic & thresholds

raw_score = (pos_words×2 + pos_emoji×1.5 + vader_pos)
          − (neg_words×2 + neg_emoji×1.5 + vader_neg)

if raw_score > 0.5 → positive
if raw_score < −0.5 → negative
else → neutral

Known assumptions & limitations

• High neutral rate (88.6% FB, 57.9% IG) is expected and appropriate: most comments on institutional pages are informational questions, general statements, or religious phrases (اللهم صلي على سيدنا محمد) which carry no valenced sentiment.

• Sarcasm and dialectal Arabic are not reliably detected. Palestinian and Levantine dialect forms may not match the MSA-dominant lexicon, slightly underestimating negative sentiment.

• Comment match rate: 45% of Facebook posts (609/1,341) and 10% of Instagram posts (179/1,775) have matched scraped comments. Posts without matched comments are excluded from sentiment aggregation — not assigned neutral by default.

• Sentiment score per post = (positive − negative) ÷ total scraped comments for that post. This is a net sentiment ratio, not an absolute count.

Planned upgrade

Once network access to HuggingFace is available in the analysis environment, the classifier will be upgraded to CAMeL-Lab/bert-base-arabic-camelbert-msa-sentiment (transformer-based, trained on 100M+ Arabic tokens). Current rule-based results are directionally reliable but should be treated as indicative, not definitive.

Data pipeline — post-to-comment merge process

Posts and comments were matched using URL-based join keys extracted from each dataset. For Facebook, the story_fbid parameter was extracted from both post_full_link (posts) and source_url (comments) using regex. For Instagram, the shortcode was extracted from /p/{shortcode}/ URLs. Posts without a matched join key in the comments file are retained in engagement analysis but excluded from sentiment aggregation. The 55% unmatched rate on Facebook and 90% on Instagram reflects the fact that the comment scrape covered a subset of high-engagement posts, not all posts in the dataset.

7  ·  Key analytical assumptions

Accepted

Public scrape data is assumed to accurately reflect the published post content and public engagement counts at the time of scraping. Minor discrepancies due to post deletions or retroactive count adjustments are considered negligible.

Accepted

Engagement is used as the primary performance proxy in the absence of reach and impressions data from public scrapes. This is standard practice in public-data social analytics and is explicitly flagged wherever reach-based metrics would give a materially different picture.

Accepted

LinkedIn engagement growth (5.6×, 2023→2025) is attributed to the quality-over-quantity content strategy shift. This is correlational, not causal — other factors (platform algorithm changes, follower growth, external events) may contribute. The finding is treated as directionally valid pending native impression data.

Accepted

The 5–8am posting concentration across all platforms is interpreted as evidence of a single scheduling tool rather than deliberate platform-specific timing. This conclusion is drawn from the near-identical hour distributions across four structurally different platforms, which would be statistically unlikely if scheduled independently.

Flagged — treat with caution

Day-of-week and posting hour analyses are computed from post timestamps. For Instagram, timestamps are UTC and converted to Palestine time (+3h standard, +2h during summer time). The conversion uses a fixed +3h offset and may introduce ±1h errors during daylight saving transitions.

Flagged — treat with caution

The Arabic sentiment classifier has not been formally evaluated against a ground-truth labelled dataset. Accuracy estimates are based on qualitative review of 20 sample comments per class during development. A formal precision/recall evaluation against 200+ labelled comments is recommended before citing sentiment figures in client-facing conclusions.

Flagged — treat with caution

YouTube view counts in the dataset reflect views at the time of scraping, not at publication. Videos published in 2013 have had 13 years to accumulate views. Comparisons across publication years are therefore not directly equivalent and should not be used to conclude that older content performs better.

Do not cite without caveat

All engagement figures derived from public scrapes should be cited as "based on public data" rather than "platform-verified." Native export figures (where available) are more reliable and should be preferred for any formal PMA reporting or tender compliance documentation.

8  ·  Reporting principles & framing

This dashboard and the associated HTML report are deliberately framed as "initial findings" rather than a comprehensive analysis. This framing is intentional and reflects the fact that private platform data access (reach, impressions, follower timelines, Stories, video completion rates) is not yet confirmed across all accounts.

The recommendations section has been withheld from the current iteration of the HTML report and dashboard. It will be reinstated once: (1) native platform exports are received for all accounts; (2) PMA strategy and brand guidelines are shared with SSA; and (3) the benchmarking accounts are provided for competitive comparison.

All data gaps are surfaced explicitly in the relevant report sections rather than silently omitted. This is a deliberate reporting principle — PMA stakeholders should be able to distinguish between findings backed by complete data and those based on partial coverage.
